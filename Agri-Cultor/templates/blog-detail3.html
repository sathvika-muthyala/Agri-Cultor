What is the real gift given by us to future generations?

Why Organic Farming?

Organic farming is a subsistence type of agricultural system that uses ecologically based pest controls and biological fertilizers derived largely from animal and plant wastes and nitrogen-fixing cover crops. It is unique production management system which promotes and enhances agro-ecosystem health, including biodiversity, biological cycles and soil biological activity.

"The health of soil, plant, animal and man is one and indivisible!!!"

The Green revolution introduced modern chemical fertilisers, modern farming techniques along with better seed management which boosted up the countryâ€™s foodgrain production. The use of chemical fertilisers, synthetic pesticides, germicides, herbicides and Genetically Engineered (GE) seeds became completely uncontrolled and started polluting the complete supply chain. As a result, children were born with disabilities and several lands became unproductive. People slowly started migrating to organic farming.

Surely we have a responsibility to leave for future generations a planet that is healthy and habitable by all species...

Thus real gift given by us to our future generations is healthy and chemical-free soil by practising organic farming. Though organic farming has a lot of challenges, we have to promote it because it produces healthy food. Crop-rotation used in organic farming strengthens the soil composition and legume family crops fix nitrogen from the air and increase the nitrates in the soil. This makes soil more fertile.

Principles of Organic Farming:

  1. Principle of Health
  2. Principle of Fairness
  3. Principle of Ecological Balance
  4. Principle of Care

Organic farming provides good quality of life and helps in reducing poverty. It uses natural resources judiciously and preserves them for future generations. Organic farming is not a new concept in India, it is time to go back to its roots and promote sustainability.
